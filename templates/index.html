Central Bank Gold Reserves
Data from World Gold Council. Figures in metric tonnes. % = share of total foreign reserves held in gold.
Why Gold Reserves Matter
Central banks hold gold as a reserve asset independent of any single currency. When countries buy gold aggressively, it signals distrust in the US dollar system. Rising central bank gold purchases → upward pressure on gold prices → gold ETFs and jewellery become more expensive. For countries like Bangladesh with high import costs, a strong dollar and high gold prices increase import bills. Countries with large gold reserves (US, Germany, Italy) have more monetary firepower in crises.
